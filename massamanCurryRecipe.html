Massaman Curry

Massaman curry is a rich, mild Thai curry with Muslim influences, made with chicken or beef, coconut milk, potatoes, onions, peanuts, and warm spices.

60 min 4.8 (95)
Dinner Intermediate

Sherri Pengjad

Ingredients in this Massaman Curry Recipe

  1. Heat the oil: Warm cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Cook the curry paste: Add Massaman curry paste. Fry for a few minutes until fragrant and slightly darkened.
  3. Add coconut milk: Pour in the coconut milk and stir until smooth. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  4. Add chicken: Add the chicken pieces. Simmer until partially cooked, about 8‚Äì10 minutes.
  5. Add potatoes and onion: Add the Yukon gold potatoes and sliced onion. Stir and return to a simmer.
  6. Add liquid if needed: Add a little water or broth if the curry looks too thick. Simmer gently until potatoes are tender.
  7. Season the curry: Add tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar. Stir to balance sour, salty, and sweet.
  8. Add peanuts: Stir in roasted peanuts. Simmer for a few more minutes so flavors meld together.
  9. Adjust and finish: Taste and adjust seasoning ‚Äî add more tamarind for tartness, more sugar for sweetness, or more fish sauce for saltiness.
  10. Serve: Ladle the curry into bowls. Garnish generously with chopped cilantro. Serve hot with steamed rice.
