Beef Buorguignon

Beef Bourguignon is a classic French stew made with tender beef slow-cooked in red wine, onions, garlic, and herbs, resulting in deep, rich flavors.

195 min 4.4 (228)

Step-by-Step Guide: Beef Buorguignon

  1. Prepare the bacon: Cut bacon or pancetta into small pieces and lightly fry until crisp. Set aside, keeping the fat in the pan.
  2. Brown the beef: Toss beef cubes in flour and brown them in the bacon fat over medium-high heat. Work in batches if needed.
  3. Saut√© vegetables: Add chopped onions, carrots, and garlic to the pan. Cook until slightly softened and aromatic.
  4. Add tomato paste and wine: Stir in tomato paste, then pour in red wine, scraping up browned bits from the pan.
  5. Add broth and herbs: Pour in beef broth until the meat is mostly covered. Add thyme, bay leaves, salt, and pepper.
  6. Simmer the stew: Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook slowly on the stovetop or in the oven until beef is tender, about 2‚Äì3 hours.
  7. Cook mushrooms and onions: In a separate pan, saut√© mushrooms and pearl onions in butter until browned, then add to the stew near the end of cooking.
  8. Combine and serve: Stir everything together, adjust seasoning if needed, and serve hot, optionally garnished with chopped parsley.
