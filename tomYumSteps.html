Tom Yum

Tom yum soup is a spicy-sour Thai soup featuring shrimp or other proteins simmered in a fragrant broth with lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves.

30 min 4.7 (265)
Dinner Intermediate

Sherri Pengjad

Step-by-Step Guide: Chicken Adobo

  1. Prepare aromatics: Trim, slice, and lightly smash lemongrass stalks. Slice galangal and thinly slice the onion. Set aside kaffir lime leaves and chilies.
  2. Heat the broth: In a large pot, bring 1 quart of chicken broth to a gentle boil over medium heat.
  3. Add lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves: Add the prepared aromatics to the boiling broth. Let them simmer for 5‚Äì10 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  4. Add onion and chilies: Stir in the sliced onion and Thai chilies. Simmer for another 3‚Äì5 minutes until the flavors meld.
  5. Add protein and vegetables (optional): You can add shrimp, chicken, mushrooms, or other preferred vegetables. Cook until protein is fully done and vegetables are tender.
  6. Season the soup: Adjust the taste with fish sauce, lime juice, or a touch of sugar if desired. Taste and balance spicy, sour, and salty flavors.
  7. Serve: Ladle the hot soup into bowls. Garnish with extra kaffir lime leaves, cilantro, or additional chilies if desired. Serve immediately.
