Chicken Enchiladas

Chicken enchiladas are Tex-Mex tortillas filled with shredded chicken, cheese, and cilantro, topped with enchilada sauce and baked.

45 min 4.8 (77)
Dinner Beginner

Lena Abraham

Step-by-step Guide: Chicken Enchiladas

  1. Prepare Veggies: Coarsely chop garlic, red bell pepper, and yellow onion. Pulse in a food processor until finely chopped. Set aside.
  2. Cook Veggies: Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chopped veggies and cook until softened. Stir in cumin and cook 1 more minute until fragrant.
  3. Make Sauce: Add enchilada sauce and crushed tomatoes to the pan. Cook until warmed through. Reserve about 3/4 cup of the sauce for topping later.
  4. Prepare Filling: In a bowl, combine shredded chicken, cilantro, half of the cheddar cheese, half of the Monterey Jack cheese, and 1 cup of the sauce mixture. Toss to combine and season with salt to taste.
  5. Assemble Enchiladas: Spread the remaining sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Slightly warm the tortillas to make them pliable. Spoon about 2/3 cup of the chicken mixture into each tortilla, roll up, and place seam-side down in the dish. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and filling. Pour reserved sauce over the top and sprinkle with the remaining cheese.
  6. Bake and Serve: Bake in a preheated oven until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Top with fresh cilantro, sour cream, lime wedges, or other desired toppings. Serve hot.
