Green Curry

Thai green curry is a spicy Thai dish made with green curry paste simmered in coconut milk, featuring proteins like chicken or shrimp and vegetables such as eggplant or bamboo shoots.

40 min 4.8 (77)
Dinner Intermediate

Sherri Pengjad

Step-by-Step Guide: Green Curry

  1. Heat the oil and coconut cream: Add cooking oil to a large pot and warm. Spoon out the thick cream from the coconut milk cans into the pot and heat over medium heat until bubbly.
  2. Cook the curry paste: Add curry paste to the coconut cream and fry for a few minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add liquid: Pour in the remaining coconut milk and water. Bring to a boil.

  4. Add meat and vegetables: Add meat and pea eggplants (if using). Return to a boil.

  5. Simmer: Reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 10 minutes.

  6. Add remaining vegetables: Add Thai eggplants, peas (fresh or frozen), and kaffir lime leaves. Simmer until tender.
  7. Season: Add fish sauce to taste. Add palm sugar if a sweeter flavor is desired.

  8. Finish with herbs: Stir in basil and cook for 1 more minute. Turn off the heat.

  9. Serve: Serve hot with steamed jasmine rice.
