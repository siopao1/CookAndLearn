Takoyaki

Is a popular Japanese street food consisting of small, round balls made from a wheat flourbased batter filled with diced octopus, pickled ginger, and green onions.

25 min 4.5 (269)
Dinner Intermediate

Namiko Hirasawa Chen

Step-by-Step Guide: Takoyaki

  1. Prep the Ingredients: Grind bonito flakes into powder. Slice scallions, mince pickled ginger, and cut boiled octopus into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Make the Batter: In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, and salt. Add eggs, soy sauce, and dashi stock, then whisk until smooth.
  3. Prepare the Pan: Preheat the takoyaki pan and grease each chamber with oil. Pour the batter into the hot chambers.
  4. Add Octopus and Toppings: Place an octopus piece into each chamber. Add bonito powder, tenkasu, scallions, and red pickled ginger.
  5. Cook and Flip: When the bottoms crisp up, use skewers to rotate each takoyaki 90 degrees. Continue turning until golden brown and evenly cooked.
  6. Assemble and Serve: Transfer takoyaki to a plate. Drizzle with takoyaki sauce and Japanese mayo. Top with katsuobushi and aonori, and serve with pickled ginger.
