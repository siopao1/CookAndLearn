Indian Samosa

Indian samosas are crispy fried pastries filled with spiced potatoes, peas, onions, and aromatic seasonings like cumin, coriander, and garam masala, served as a popular savory snack.

60 min 4.7 (232)
Lunch Intermediate

Kimber

Step-by-Step Guide: Indian Samosa

  1. Boil the Potatoes: Place potato cubes in a pot of cold water, stir in salt, and bring to a boil. Simmer until tender but still holding shape, about 15 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare Spice Mix: In a small bowl, combine 1 teaspoon kosher salt, garam masala, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and cayenne.
  3. Cook Aromatics and Spices: Heat olive oil and butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add serrano, garlic, ginger, and spice mix. Stir for 2 minutes until fragrant. Add peas and cook 3 more minutes.
  4. Combine with Potatoes: Transfer the saut√©ed mixture to a bowl with the cooked potatoes. Add cilantro and lemon juice. Mix gently, keeping some potatoes partially mashed. Adjust salt to taste.
  5. Make Yogurt Chutney: Combine cilantro, mint, green onions, serrano, lime juice, and yogurt in a blender. Blend until smooth. Season with salt.
  6. Assemble Quesadillas: Spread a few spoonfuls of the potato filling over one half of a tortilla. Fold over to cover the filling. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
  7. Cook Quesadillas: Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook each quesadilla for 2‚Äì3 minutes per side until golden and crispy.
  8. Serve: Cut each quesadilla into thirds. Serve warm or at room temperature with the yogurt chutney.
