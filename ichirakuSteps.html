Homemade Ichiraku

Rich and flavorful ramen soup with tender pork, soft-boiled eggs, and fresh vegetables

50 min 4.9 (218)
Dinner Intermediate

Danielle Baghernejad

Step-by-Step Guide: Homemade Ichiraku

  1. Prepare the Broth: Rinse chicken bones and place them in a large pot with water, pork, garlic, ginger, and green onions. Bring to a boil, skim impurities, then lower heat and simmer covered for about two hours.
  2. Strain the Broth: Remove the pork and strain the broth, discarding the solids.

  3. Season the Broth: Add soy sauce, sake, and mirin. Simmer covered until ready to serve.

  4. Braise the Chashu Pork: Combine chashu sauce ingredients in a large pan. Add pork, bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 30 minutes, turning occasionally until only a small amount of liquid remains.
  5. Finish the Chashu: Remove the lid and simmer until bubbles appear. Turn the pork to coat it in the sauce, then remove and slice thinly.
  6. Cook the Noodles: Prepare ramen noodles according to package instructions.

  7. Assemble the Ramen Bowls: Divide the broth into serving bowls, add noodles, sliced chashu, bamboo shoots, soy-marinated eggs, and green onions.
  8. Serve Hot: Enjoy your homemade Ichiraku-style ramen immediately.
