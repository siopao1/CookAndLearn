Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo is a classic Filipino dish made by simmering chicken in a savory-sour sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns.

40 min 4.8 (234)
Lunch Intermediate

Carl John Tolenada

Ingredients in this Chicken Adobo Recipe

  1. Chicken: slice the chicken into serving pieces before cooking. The cuts are usually smaller.
  2. Bay leaves: This is an essential ingredient for Filipino adobo as far as I am concerned. Dried bay leaves (locally called "dahon ng laurel") are usually used because it is available year round.
  3. Soy sauce: I personally prefer Filipino brand soy sauce in making adobo. I have nothing against Kikkoman and other Asian brands, but the dish will taste more authentic if local soy sauce is used.
  4. Vinegar: White vinegar is the most common type to use for adobo. Sometimes I use cane or rice vinegar too.
  5. Garlic: Adobo should be garlicy. The garlic can be sauteed and boiled just like in this recipe, or it can be toasted.
  6. Sugar: this is an optional ingredient. Sugar is added to balance the saltiness of the dish. You can add more too if you want your chicken adobo to be sweet side.
  7. Salt: This is optional because the soy sauce is already salty to begin with. I only add salt when needed, which is not usual.
  8. Peppercorn: It does not matter if it is whole, crushed, or ground. Whole peppercorn has just been used traditionally.
