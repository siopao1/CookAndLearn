Chalupas

Chalupas are Mexican snacks with crispy masa shells, filled with seasoned beef, beans, cheese, and salsa.

45 min 4.6 (232)
Lunch Intermediate

Francesca Zani

Step-by-step Guide: Chalupas

  1. Prepare Salsa Verde: Roast tomatillos until softened and slightly charred. Blend with onion, garlic, cilantro, lime juice, and salt until smooth. Add extra lime juice or water if needed to reach desired consistency. Set aside.
  2. Cook Ground Beef: In a pan over medium heat, cook ground beef with kosher salt, chili powder, dried Mexican oregano, ground coriander, and ground cumin until browned and cooked through. Set aside.
  3. Prepare Dough: Combine masa harina, flour, baking powder, and salt. Mix in cold butter until small pebbles form. Slowly add milk until a dough forms. Shape into a ball and let rest 10 minutes.
  4. Shape and Prick Dough: Divide the dough into 6 pieces. Roll each into a small circle and prick with a fork to prevent air bubbles.
  5. Fry Chalupas: Heat vegetable oil in a pan. Using tongs, lower half of a dough circle into the oil until firm, then release the other half so it folds into a taco-shell shape. Fry until golden-brown, turning once. Drain on paper towels. Repeat with the remaining dough.
  6. Assemble Chalupas: Fill fried chalupa shells with cooked ground beef and optional black beans. Serve with salsa verde on the side.
