Tamales

Tamales are Mexican steamed masa packets filled with meats like chicken or pork, wrapped in corn husks or parchment.

180 min 4.8 (92)
Breakfast Advanced

Stacey Ballis

Step-by-step Guide: Tamales

  1. Prepare Masa: Dissolve salt in hot water and add to masa harina. Mix until no dry pockets remain. Let rest 20‚Äì30 minutes to hydrate.
  2. Whip Fat: In a mixer, beat shortening or lard with baking powder for 3 minutes to aerate. Gradually add hydrated masa until fully incorporated.
  3. Add Broth: Stream in chicken or vegetable broth until mixture is smooth. Batter should be thick but spreadable. Cover and refrigerate 1‚Äì3 hours. Beat 2 minutes before using.
  4. Assemble Tamales: Place a scoop of masa in the center of a parchment square or corn husk. Spread, leaving 2 inches at bottom, 0.5 inch at top, and 1 inch on sides. Add filling in the center. Fold sides over filling, roll, and pinch bottom to secure. Top remains open for steaming.
  5. Steam Tamales: Fill a pot with 2 inches of water and bring to a simmer. Place steamer basket inside. Stand tamales upright, open ends up. Fill empty spaces with foil if needed. Steam 60‚Äì75 minutes.
  6. Serve: Remove tamales from parchment and serve hot.
