Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a Thai stir-fried dish made with flat rice noodles, egg, shrimp or tofu, bean sprouts, and chives, flavored with tamarind, fish sauce, and sugar, and topped with lime and crushed peanuts.

20 min 4.6 (92)
Dinner Intermediate

Sherri Pengjad

Step-by-Step Guide: Pad Thai

  1. Cook the noodles: Follow package instructions until tender, then rinse under cold water to stop cooking.

  2. Make the sauce: In a bowl, stir together fish sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar (or tamarind paste), Sriracha, and peanut butter if using. Set aside.

  3. Saut√© protein and veggies: Heat 1¬Ω Tbsp oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add shrimp, chicken, or tofu, garlic, and bell pepper. Cook until protein is fully done.

  4. Cook the eggs: Push ingredients to the side, add a little more oil, then pour in beaten eggs. Scramble, breaking into small pieces.

  5. Combine and toss: Add noodles, sauce, bean sprouts, and peanuts (reserve some for topping). Toss until evenly coated.

  6. Serve: Top with green onions, extra peanuts, cilantro, and lime wedges. Serve immediately.
