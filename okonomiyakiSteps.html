Okonomiyaki

Okonomiyaki is a Japanese teppanyaki savory pancake dish consisting of wheat flour batter and other ingredients cooked on a teppan.

35 min 4.7 (174)
Lunch Beginner

Namiko Hirasawa Chen

Step-by-Step Guide: Okonomiyaki

  1. Make the Base Batter: Mix flour, grated yam, and dashi into a smooth batter. Let it rest briefly to enhance flavor and improve fluffiness.
  2. Prep the Ingredients: Finely chop cabbage, slice green onions, and prepare proteins or add-ins. Drain the cabbage well to prevent thinning the batter.
  3. Mix the Batter: Add eggs, tempura scraps (tenkasu), and chopped red pickled ginger to the base batter. Gently fold in the cabbage until evenly coated.
  4. Cook the Okonomiyaki: Heat a lightly oiled frying pan or griddle over medium heat. Spoon the batter into a round pancake about ¬æ inch thick. Place pork belly slices or other toppings on top. Cook until the bottom is golden, flip, and continue cooking until both sides are browned and the center is cooked through.
  5. Add Condiments and Toppings: Transfer to a plate, brush with okonomiyaki sauce, drizzle with Japanese mayonnaise, and sprinkle aonori and katsuobushi. Serve hot.
