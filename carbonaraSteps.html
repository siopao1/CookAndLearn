Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is a creamy Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, crispy bacon, and black pepper, creating a rich and comforting flavor.

25 min 4.5 (269)
Dinner Beginner

Carl John Tolenada

Step-by-Step Guide: Pork Sinigang

  1. Cook the pancetta or guanciale: In a large skillet over medium heat, cook pancetta or guanciale until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside, reserving the rendered fat.
  2. Boil the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente, then drain, reserving 1 cup of pasta water.
  3. Mix the eggs and cheese: In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, whole egg, and finely grated Pecorino Romano. Season with a pinch of freshly ground black pepper.
  4. Combine pasta and pancetta: Return the skillet with the pancetta to low heat, add the drained spaghetti, and toss to coat in the rendered fat.
  5. Add egg mixture: Remove skillet from heat. Quickly pour egg and cheese mixture over pasta, tossing continuously to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time if needed.
  6. Serve: Garnish with extra Pecorino Romano and freshly ground black pepper before serving.
